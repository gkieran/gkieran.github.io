Economy

Considering that this country is located in Africa, it's quite common to assume that it is poor, that it has been poor, and that it will continue to be poor. And sure, a quick search on Google will verify that assumption. That is only one perspective of the economy of the country, however. As a matter of fact, Benin had a prosperous economy that was fueled by the trade of slaves and natural resources.
There were several trade relationships established between Benin artisans and traders along with the Portuguese, which resulted in large profits for Benin. Benin's artwork, gold, ivory, and pepper are among the things that the Portuguese bought consistently from the country. Another economic success of Benin, although quite cruel, was the African slave trade. Benin was heavily involved in this practice, constantly selling men, women, and children to nations like the early United States.
