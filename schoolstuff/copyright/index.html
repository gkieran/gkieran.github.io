Copyright and Fair Use

What is Copyright?

  • Legal protection provided to creators of original content (photos/videos, music, writing, etc)
  • Grants the holder exclusive rights to display, sell, perform, etc, their work

What is Protected?

  • Literary works (books, poems, articles, etc)
  • Artistic works (drawings, paintings, photographs, etc)
  • Musical works (songs, scores, etc)
  • Dramatic works (plays, screenplays, scripts, etc)
  • Movies/Audiovisual works

What isn't Protected?

  • Facts, ideas, procedures, processes, systems, methods of operation
  • Works in the public domain
  • US government works

The Fair Use Doctrine

  • Allows limited use of copyrighted material without permission for purposes like criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, and research
  • Four factors considered
    • Purpose and character of use
    • Nature of the copyrighted work
    • Amount used
    • Effect on market for original work

Fair Use Examples

  • Quoting from a book in a book review
  • Using a short clip from a movie in a documentary
  • Parodying elements of a popular song or movie
